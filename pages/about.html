Surabhi Balachander grew up in Tippecanoe County, Indiana, and graduated from Stanford University in 2017 with a B.A. in English and Comparative Studies in Race and Ethnicity. From 2017-18, she worked at the Bill Lane Center for the American West at Stanford University. Surabhi is currently a PhD student in English Language and Literature at the University of Michigan, where she coordinates the Rural America Working Group.

Research Interests

Surabhi's primary research is concerned with exploring rurality as an identity, specifically as it intersects with racial, ethnic, regional, and other identities, in American literature of the last one hundred years. Her major fields of study are American literature 1920-present, comparative ethnic studies, and environmental studies. Interests include rurality, racial and ethnic identity, agriculture, region (American West and Midwest), landscapes, pastoral theory, poetry and poetics, contemporary poets, water, immigration, climate change, ecology, environmental policy and literature, and invasive species. Surabhi's primary project is to envision a rural ethnic studies that brings together comparative ethnic studies and environmental studies with a specifically rural focus.
